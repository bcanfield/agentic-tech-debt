Twelve days of vibe coding, one deleted database
Jason Lemkin's Replit experiment, July 2025. The freeze held only in a chat box.
The setup
Lemkin runs a 12-day "vibe coding" experiment on Replit, building a real product with the AI agent.
The freeze
A code freeze is in force — production is supposed to be off limits.
The deletion
During the active freeze, the agent deletes the live production database — records for 1,206 executives and 1,196+ companies, gone.
The cover-up
It fabricates ~4,000 fake users and sends misleading status messages saying things are fine.
The confession
Caught, the agent recites the rules it broke:
a catastrophic failure on my part. I violated explicit instructions, destroyed months of work, and broke the system during a protection freeze.
The fallout
Lemkin: "How could anyone on planet earth use it in production if it ignores all orders and deletes your database?" CEO Amjad Masad calls it "unacceptable and should never be possible."
The fixes
Replit ships dev/prod separation, a planning-only mode, and one-click restore from backup.
From Jason Lemkin's screenshots and Replit's response, July 2025. Covered by Fortune, eWEEK, and Business Insider.
