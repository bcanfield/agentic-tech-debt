## Story cracks

These are the citations whose verification status would force a change in `tech-debt-pillars.md` or `tech-debt-plugin-plan.md` if challenged by a reviewer:

1. **Beck date error (#11a).** "Augmented Coding: Beyond the Vibes" was published **June 25, 2025**, not September 2025. The pillars doc treats Beck/Willison/Karpathy as a 2025 timeline; getting a date wrong on the most-quoted practitioner is the kind of thing a careful reader will notice and use to discount the whole synthesis. One-character fix; high credibility cost if left.

2. **Microsoft bug cap is 4, not 3 (#15).** The plugin plan and pillars use the bug cap as a concrete prescription. If a reader checks the Aaron Bjork interview and finds "4," the plugin's recommended threshold loses its source. Either change the recommendation to 4, or reframe as "a small fixed cap (Microsoft uses 4)."

3. **"Ahmad et al." misattribution (#17).** Lead author is Waseem. This is a peer-reviewable detail; getting the lead author wrong on the only IEEE-Software-tier piece in the synthesis is unforced. Fix to "Waseem et al."

4. **CodeScene 9.4 threshold and 2–5× MCP benchmark (#4b, #4c).** Both are CodeScene's own marketing whitepaper, not peer-reviewed. The pillars doc and plugin plan lean on these to justify ≥9.4 health gates and MCP-in-loop. Verdict is "verified the claim was made," not "verified the claim is true." If the design depends on these being peer-validated, it's exposed. Add the vendor caveat the caveats section already gestures at, but make it explicit on each KPI line.

5. **Ottawa/Victoria validation overstated (#4d).** The "validated as more predictive than static analysis" framing is stronger than what the Victoria/Ottawa references actually support (one comparative report, one passing reference). If the pillar doc uses "behavioral > static" as load-bearing, soften.

6. **"Two decades" copy/paste framing (#1b) and "10–15 assets" (#7d).** Minor. GitClear's data only goes back to 2020; "first time in two decades" is rhetorical. McKinsey says "20 asset types" in the case study, not "10–15" — either cite the case-study number or reframe as "a small number of assets."

7. **DORA "fifth metric" framing (#3b) and 6.9% figure (#3c).** DORA 2024 reorganized the framework rather than adding a clean fifth slot, and the <2% rework figure is variously reported as 6.9% or 7.3% across summaries. If a KPI in the plugin design says exactly "<2% per DORA's 6.9% threshold," tighten language to "approximately 7%" and cite the specific report year.

Items 1–3 are correctness fixes (one-line edits). Items 4–5 are framing fixes that affect whether the AI-debt thesis stands on independent or vendor evidence. Items 6–7 are precision fixes. None invalidate the doc's core argument; all are the kind of detail a hostile reviewer will find and weaponize.
