# Article Headlines: AI Tech Debt Content Pipeline

Master list of distinct article ideas derived from [ai-tech-debt-stories.md](./ai-tech-debt-stories.md).
Generated by five parallel brainstorms (incidents, data, thesis, personas, how-to), then deduped —
where multiple angles hit the same anchor, they're grouped so we never publish two articles that read the same.

Each entry: **headline** — angle — *anchor source* — `persona` where targeted.

## Pillar 1 — The big thesis (cornerstone essays)

1. **Code Is Now Cheap to Write and Expensive to Own** — the economics-inversion pillar piece; every practice built on "writing is the hard part" is obsolete. — *Gauge "penalty for a high-debt codebase is larger than ever"*
2. **Verification Debt Is the Only Debt That Matters Now** — name the new debt class: the gap between generation speed and human validation speed. — *Vogels, re:Invent 2025*
3. **Tech Debt Isn't the Problem. Invisible Debt Is.** — debt you record is a financing instrument; debt nobody wrote down is a landmine, and agents mint it at machine speed. — *Gartner "AI debt will compound left unaddressed"* — the most on-brand piece for debt-ops
4. **Stop Asking If AI Code Is Good. Ask If Anyone Decided Anything.** — debt is deferred *decisions*; agents defer them silently by default. — *Edward Anderson Jr. (Texas McCombs)*
5. **Review Is the New Compile Step** — review went from formality to throttle to the only control point; it needs tooling like compilation got tooling. (alt headline: *"Code Review Is the New Build Queue"*) — *Vogels + OX "review cannot scale"*
6. **AI Didn't Lower the Bar. It Removed the Throttle.** — quality gates were always weak; they were just protected by how slow writing code was. — *OX "Army of Juniors"*
7. **Registering Debt Beats Preventing It** — prevention-first culture loses to AI throughput math; Fowler's prudent-deliberate quadrant at scale is the winning move. — *Fowler quadrant + McKinsey complexity finding*
8. **In Defense of Vibe Coding** — contrarian: vibe coding isn't reckless, *unaccounted* vibe coding is. — *Vogels "vibe coding is fine, but…"*
9. **You Build It, You Own It — Even When the Agent Built It** — ownership doesn't transfer to the model; it now requires an audit trail of what the agent decided for you. — *Vogels*
10. **Your Quarterly Cleanup Sprint Is Dead** — batch-mode debt management assumed human-speed accrual; at agent speed it's theater. — *GitClear refactoring collapse (−39.9% moved lines)*
11. **The Tech Debt Reckoning Will Not Be a Crash. It Will Be a Slow Leak.** — no headline catastrophe; compounding churn and comprehension loss quietly halve velocity. — *MIT Sloan*
12. **We Saw This Coming in 2014 — We Just Called It Machine Learning** — the Sculley "high-interest credit card" lineage playing out at industry scale. — *NIPS 2014/2015 papers*
13. **Nobody Understands the Codebase Anymore — and That's the Plan** — comprehension/cognitive debt: build systems that record what no human read. — *O'Reilly "junior generates faster than senior can audit" + Storey*
14. **Automation Bias Is the New Root Cause** — the next five years of postmortems trace to humans rubber-stamping plausible code. — *Gartner Predicts 2026*
15. **Debt, Not Tokens, Is the Limiting Factor on Agent Velocity** — teams obsess over models and context windows; the real governor is unmanaged debt drag. — *Gauge + DORA 2025*
16. **Maintenance Is Becoming a Specialist Profession — and We're Starving It** — AI concentrates maintenance on a shrinking expert pool nobody headcount-plans for. — *Tilburg (arXiv 2510.10165)*
17. **Citizen Developers Are About to Learn What Technical Debt Means** — prompt-to-app hands non-engineers debt accrual with none of the cultural antibodies. — *Gartner 2,500% prediction*
18. **The Felt-Faster Fallacy: Why Your Team's AI Velocity Is a Vibe** — perceived and actual speed have decoupled; the gap is where unmeasured debt hides. — *METR (as epistemics essay, not stat piece)*

## Sequencing (per the research doc's recommendations)

- **Start (Stage 1):** pair one stat piece with one incident piece — #45 (METR) + #19 or #20 (Replit), or #40 (Veracode) + #33 (curl). A/B which hook style wins.
- **Pillar piece (Stage 2):** #1 or #2, with #52/#53 as the data spine and #54 as the forward-looking close.
- **Segment (Stage 3):** the persona variants (#74–82) are the distribution layer for the pillar content.
- **Product positioning (Stage 4):** every Pillar 4 piece funnels directly; #56 is the anchor.
- **Hedges:** if DORA 2026 turns positive on stability, lean on the GitClear/comprehension pieces (#37–39, #13) over the instability pieces (#46). If model security improves, shift from #40-class headlines to maintainability ones.

## Caveats carried over from the research doc

Cite vendor studies (Veracode, Apiiro, OX, CodeRabbit, GitClear, Uplevel) as vendor research. The Amazon 6.3M figure
is a Business Insider estimate Amazon disputes — always attribute. METR's n=16 with early-2025 models. Verify
incident-catalog entries against primary reporting before publishing. Correlation ≠ causation on GitClear/DORA.
